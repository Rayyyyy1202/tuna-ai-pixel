## 1. Brand Story

### Tuna never stop swimming.

They have no swim bladder. They breathe by ram ventilation — water must pass through their gills, constantly, or they suffocate. Movement isn't discipline. It's how they exist.

**Keep moving. Or stop existing.**

We think real AI agents work the same way.

---

### The problem

Enterprises are paying six and seven figures for AI that waits to be prompted, answers in isolation, and forgets the moment the conversation ends.

That's not an agent. That's a chatbot in a suit.

Meanwhile, the work that actually matters — designing systems, writing production code, building internal tools, automating workflows — still falls back on human teams, long timelines, and consulting bills that don't match the output.

---

### What TUNA.AI does

We build AI agents that design and ship software for enterprises.

- **Ship in days, not quarters.**
- **From brief to build.**
- **Outcomes, not deliverables.**

Give us a problem. We hand you the working thing — not a recommendation, not a deck, not a prototype that dies in staging.

---

### Why we'll win

Most AI products are built like goldfish: they wait, they respond, they idle.

We built ours like tuna. Always moving. Always sensing. Always closing the gap between problem and solution.

A tuna travels 10,000+ kilometers a year. It's warm-blooded in cold water. It's the ocean's most efficient predator — not because it's the biggest, but because it never stops.

That's the kind of agent enterprises need.
That's what we ship.

## 4. Founder Story

### Why we built TUNA.AI

We've spent the last few years on the inside of enterprise AI — and we kept seeing the same thing.

Companies were spending millions on AI initiatives that ended in slide decks. Pilots that never reached production. Prototypes that worked in demos and broke in staging. Consultants who delivered "strategy" and left the building before anything shipped.

The technology was ready. The delivery model wasn't.

So we built a different one.

---

Ray has spent his career shipping software that actually runs in production. He's built systems used by millions of users and worked with AI in the trenches long before it was a category. If TUNA.AI ships in days instead of quarters, it's because Ray refuses to accept that software has to be slow.

**Wellness Wei** — *AI advisor.*
Wellness has sat across the table from dozens of enterprise leaders asking the same question: *how do we actually use this?* She's seen which AI projects deliver ROI and which become expensive science experiments. She makes sure TUNA.AI is on the right side of that line — every time.

---

### Why now

Foundation models are good enough. Tooling has caught up. The bottleneck isn't intelligence anymore — it's **delivery**.

The next decade of enterprise software won't be won by whoever has the best model. It'll be won by whoever can take a problem and put a working solution in front of the customer the fastest.

That's the company we're building.

**Keep moving. Or stop existing.**

## 5. Usage Guide

### Voice & tone

- **Direct, not decorative.** Short sentences. Strong verbs. No throat-clearing.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** State what we do. Don't oversell.
- **Opinionated, not preachy.** We have a point of view about how AI should be delivered. We don't lecture.
- **Concrete, not abstract.** "Ship in days" beats "accelerate timelines." Always.

### Words we use

ship · build · deliver · move · agent · production · outcome · solve

### Words we avoid

leverage · synergize · empower · revolutionary · cutting-edge · seamless · holistic · solution provider

### The metaphor — when to use it, when to retire it

The tuna metaphor is the brand's spine, but it should never be over-explained. Use it once per surface — once on the homepage, once in the deck, once in the keynote. After that, the product does the talking.

If a customer asks "why tuna?" — that's the moment for the full story. If they don't ask, don't volunteer it.
